The Rayleigh Resolution Limit

The fundamental constraint of optical lithography is governed by the Rayleigh Criterion. ASML bypasses the limits of DUV (193nm) by transitioning to 13.5nm EUV photons.

\[ CD = k_1 \frac{\lambda}{NA} \]

Where:
\( CD \): Critical Dimension (Resolution)
\( k_1 \): Process capability factor (\(\approx 0.25\) physical limit)
\( \lambda \): Wavelength (13.5 nm)
\( NA \): Numerical Aperture (0.33 standard, 0.55 High-NA)

By decreasing \(\lambda\) from 193nm to 13.5nm (a 14x reduction), ASML enables the printing of features that were previously theoretically impossible.

02. Plasma Physics & EUV Generation

The EUV light source is not a bulb, but a continuous series of controlled explosions. Molten tin (Sn) droplets are fired at 70 meters per second, hit twice by a CO2 laser.

01

Pre-Pulse Impact

A low-intensity laser pulse hits the 30μm tin droplet, flattening it into a pancake shape to increase surface area for the main pulse.

02

Main-Pulse Ionization

A high-power (20kW) CO2 laser pulse vaporizes the tin, heating it to ~350,000 Kelvin, creating a highly ionized plasma.

Proof: Peak Emission Wavelength

Wien's Displacement Law Approximation: \[ \lambda_{max} \approx \frac{b}{T} \] For Tin (Sn) \(4d-4f\) transitions, the EUV peak occurs at: \[ E_{photon} = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \frac{1240 \text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}}{13.5 \text{ nm}} \approx 91.8 \text{ eV} \] Plasma Temperature required: \[ k_B T \approx 30 \text{ eV} \implies T \approx 3.5 \times 10^5 \text{ K} \]

03. High-NA Reflective Optics

Since all matter absorbs 13.5nm light, traditional lenses are obsolete. ASML uses ultra-precise mirrors made by Zeiss, composed of molybdenum and silicon bi-layers.

Bragg Reflection Synthesis

Reflectivity is achieved via constructive interference across 50-100 layer pairs. Each layer is ~3.4nm thick—precisely half the EUV wavelength.

Bragg's Law for Multi-layers: \[ n\lambda = 2d \sin \theta \sqrt{1 - \frac{2\bar{\delta}}{\sin^2 \theta}} \] Where:
\( d = d_{Si} + d_{Mo} \approx 6.9 \text{ nm} \)
\( \bar{\delta} \): Mean refractive index decrement (\(\approx 0.03\))
Peak Reflectivity \( R_{max} \approx 70\% \text{ per mirror} \)

Quantum Precision Requirement

Surface roughness must be less than 0.05 nm. If the mirror were the size of Germany, the highest mountain would be less than 1 millimeter tall.

04. Mechatronics: Stage Dynamics

The wafer stage must move with nanometer accuracy while accelerating at 7g. ASML uses a dual-stage system: one wafer is exposed while the other is measured.

Control Theory & Maglev Proof

Magnetic levitation eliminates friction. Positioning is maintained via 6-degree-of-freedom (6-DOF) Lorentz actuators.

Lorentz Force Control: \[ \mathbf{F} = \int (\mathbf{J} \times \mathbf{B}) dV \] Dynamic Positioning Error (\(\epsilon\)) minimization: \[ \min \int_0^T [ \epsilon(t)^2 + \rho u(t)^2 ] dt \] Current ASML specification:
Overlay Accuracy: \( < 1.0 \text{ nm} \)
Scan Speed: \( 500 \text{ mm/s} \)

Vacuum Interaction

Stage movement creates acoustic vibrations even in vacuum (transferred via the frame). Passive and active isolation systems (Air mounts + Piezo) cancel 99.99% of jitter.

05. Resists & Secondary Electrons

A single 92eV EUV photon doesn't just hit one molecule; it triggers a cascade of secondary electrons that do the heavy lifting of chemical transformation.

The Shot Noise Problem (Poisson Statistics)

At 13.5nm, the number of photons per unit area is low. \[ \sigma_N = \sqrt{N} \implies \text{Relative Uncertainty} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \] This leads to Line Edge Roughness (LER). To reduce LER by 2x, you need 4x the dose (\(N\)), which slows down throughput.

Resist Domains

CAR (Chemically Amplified Resists): Use a photoacid generator (PAG). One photon -> One acid -> Many chemical reactions.

MOR (Metal-Oxide Resists): Higher absorption cross-section, tighter electron blur, but requires higher doses.

Dill's Equations for Exposure

\[ \frac{\partial I(z,t)}{\partial z} = -I(z,t) [A M(z,t) + B] \] \[ \frac{\partial M(z,t)}{\partial t} = -I(z,t) M(z,t) C \] Where \( M \) is the concentration of inhibitors, \( A, B, C \) are Dill's parameters.

06. Computational Holography

The mask is not a literal image of the circuit. It is a "distorted" version (Inverse Lithography) designed to counteract the physics of diffraction.

OPC (Optical Proximity Correction)

Features on the mask include tiny "serifs" and "hammerheads" to prevent corner rounding.


ILT (Inverse Lithography Technology)

Treating the lithography system as a mathematical operator \(\mathcal{F}\) and solving the inverse problem:

\[ \text{Mask} = \arg \min_{M} \| \text{Target} - \mathcal{F}(M) \|^2 + \alpha \text{Reg}(M) \] This requires massive GPU clusters to compute patterns for a single chip.

08. The High-NA Future

The next jump is the EXE:5000 series. Increasing the NA from 0.33 to 0.55 enables 1.7x better resolution.

The Anamorphic Challenge

High-NA mirrors require Anamorphic Optics—different magnification in X and Y (4x vs 8x). This prevents the incident light from hitting the mask at too steep an angle (shadowing effect).

Mathematical Contrast (Image Log-Slope)

\[ ILS = \frac{d \ln I}{dx} \] High-NA increases the slope of the intensity profile, allowing for thinner resists and higher aspect ratio features.
